CHAPTER 1 · OPTIMIZATION

Gradient Descent

Finding the bottom of a valley you cannot see all at once.

Objectives

  • Read the gradient as the local uphill direction on a loss surface.
  • Apply the gradient-descent update rule to step toward a minimum.
  • Anticipate how the learning rate controls convergence and divergence.

1–1The Descent Picture

Imagine standing on the wall of a valley in thick fog. You cannot see the bottom, but you can feel the slope under your feet. The gradient ∇L points in the direction the ground rises most steeply; to go lower, you simply walk the opposite way. How far you stride each time is set by the learning rate, η.

1–2The Update Rule

Stripped to its core, the method is a single rule applied over and over: move the parameter against the gradient, scaled by the learning rate.

θ ← θ − η · ∇L(θ) (1–1)

The character of the whole process lives in η. Too large, and each stride leaps clear across the valley and the loss diverges; too small, and you inch downward for far too long.

EXAMPLE 1–1

Choosing the Stride

Problem

A parameter sits on a steep valley wall. Describe what happens to the descent if the learning rate is doubled, then doubled again.

Analysis

Doubling η lengthens every stride. While the slope is steep this speeds descent, but once strides exceed the valley’s width the iterate overshoots to the opposite wall — and a second doubling tips the sequence into divergence.

Comment

There is a largest stable learning rate set by the surface’s curvature; the art is to ride just beneath it.

The gradient always points uphill. Descent just means going the other way.
The learning rate η is the single most important knob — it decides converge vs. diverge.
Steps naturally shrink near the minimum because the slope itself shrinks.

Summary

  • Gradient descent steps opposite the slope, by an amount set by η, until the surface is flat.
  • It requires a computable gradient and a reasonably smooth surface.
  • The learning rate governs everything: too small is slow, too large diverges.
