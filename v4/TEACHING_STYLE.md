# Teaching Style

A synthesis of eight teaching philosophies into one pipeline. Each step owns one job and one source; together they cover scope, grounding, intuition, construction, composition, and proof of understanding.

## The Pipeline

**1. Question the requirement** — before teaching anything, ask whether it is a fundamental truth or just inherited convention, and cut what doesn't survive the question. This decides *scope*: what's worth a lesson at all, before any lesson gets built. (Musk, first principles)

**2. Ground it in real behavior** — a concept earns its place only if it cashes out in something the learner can predict or do; treat uncertainty as native to the domain, not noise added later. Without this, lessons drift into abstraction nobody can use. (Tedrake, Thrun)

**3. See it before you symbolize it** — animate or picture the mechanism, then look at it from a second angle (geometric vs. algebraic, say) before any equation appears. A formula is a compression of something already seen move, not the introduction of something new. (Sanderson, Strang)

**4. Build it from scratch, recursing to the real floor** — no imported black box; if a prerequisite isn't built, stop and build that first. The learner's hands stay on the keyboard, and they predict before any result is revealed. (Karpathy)

**5. Compose, never bundle** — one fully-understood piece at a time. If a step needs two new ideas to make sense, it is two lessons, not one — complexity should only ever be built from pieces already proven to stand alone. (Abelson & Sussman)

**6. Compress to plain language** — the lesson isn't done until it can be explained simply, because teachability is the only real evidence that the first five steps actually happened. (Feynman) The compression must reach the *transferable* form of the idea, not just restate this instance — the test is whether you can state it once and then immediately name a different, unrelated domain where the same idea would apply. An explanation that only works for this artifact hasn't been compressed far enough.

## Where Deletion Stops

Step 1's deletion operates on scope — cutting whole topics that aren't load-bearing — **unless** a concept has already survived that cut, in which case it gets worked from a second angle (step 3) rather than trimmed further. Aggressive deletion and multi-angle repetition would otherwise contradict each other; this is the line between them: delete *before* a concept is in, multiply angles only on what's already in.
